# Raree Show Constitution

## AI Summary

Raree Show helps people understand complex stories in minutes while
preserving the original narrative experience.

It achieves this by reducing cognitive cost through progressive
capability convergence rather than feature accumulation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 1. Introduction

Raree Show is a new medium for understanding complex narratives.

This Constitution defines the principles that should remain stable as
Raree Show continues to evolve.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. Why

## Problem

Complex stories contain intertwined characters, events, locations, and
timelines. Building a coherent mental model often requires significant
time and repeated reading.

## Mission

Reduce the cognitive cost of understanding complex stories while
preserving the author's narrative experience.

## Vision

Enable a new medium for understanding complex narratives through
AI-assisted experiences.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 3. What

Raree Show is defined by the experience it delivers and the principles
that preserve that experience.

## Core Experience

Enable readers to naturally build a coherent mental model of a complex
story within minutes.

## Core Principles

Every product decision should strengthen the reader's understanding
while protecting the integrity of the original narrative.

``` text
Philosophy
      ↓
Decision
      ↓
Execution
```

### Philosophy

The beliefs that define why Raree Show is built this way.

-   Cognitive Cost First
-   Reader Understanding First
-   Preserve the Author's Narrative Experience

### Decision

The principles that translate philosophy into product choices.

-   Mission Before Features
-   Convergence Before Expansion
-   Optimize Globally, Not Locally

### Execution

The practices that translate decisions into working software.

-   One Capability at a Time
-   Evidence Before Completion
-   Build on Dependencies
